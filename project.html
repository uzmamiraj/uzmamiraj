Sana Mir- Biography of Pakistan Female Cricket National Team Captain

Women’s cricket is getting more and more popularity and the national team of Pakistan can be commended for making a big progress from its founding, as until today it is managed to win two consecutive gold medals at Asian Games. She has no formal training since 1990s there rarely was any cricket clubs for women however her love blossomed for the game in street and alleys of Pakistan. From a dusty girl in the rural streets to someone who has found a spot in the list of glamorous women cricketers she has seen it all. She an all-rounder as she is a left armed bowler and right handed batsman. Mir made the ODI debut in 2005 in the game against Sri Lanka National team and went on to show her full potential in 2008 when she put in a brilliant performance at the 2008’s women cricket World Cup qualifier, where she was give the Player of Tournament Award. Pakistani national side played in the Asian Games and managed to go all the way and win a gold medal. The team repeated this success for four years later when they won another Asian Games gold medal in Incheon, South Korea.



  • Sana Mir’s Early and Adult life

Talking about her personal life she has moved place to place in Pakistan a lot. Her family background is that of a small humble family from Abbottabad. Her parents are Kashmiri, her father name is Mir Moatazid who served in the Pakistani army as an Army officer and hence the family had to move a lot. She has three siblings and she was always supported by her family. She wasn’t different form other five years old girls, playing cricket with brothers on street and trying to copying styles of cricketing idols but today she is the most successful captains in women cricket. In one of her interviews she said that ‘when she moves to a new place and when go to play with the boys they first took her trial through putting her for fielding and when she gives her best then they gave her a chance of batting, that’s how she learned a lot from the boys too’.


  • Sana Mir’s Cricketing Career and Achievements

Sana started playing cricket at a very young age and didn’t get any formal training. Before her rise to international lever she used to play at domestic cricket from Zarai Tarqiati Bank Limited, Pakistan Cricket Board Women Greens and South Zone Women Karachi. Her profile is quite strong. Her debut match in ODI category was on the 28th December, 2005 against Sri Lanka. She has played 66 ODI matches and has scored total ODI runs of 766. Her top score is 49 till now and her T20 stats a pretty admirable as she led her team to the first ever tournament to win in the categories of ODI and T20.



  • Challenges faced by Sana Mir

She didn’t have any first-hand experience of any kind of social barriers but, she have witnessed other girls struggling a lot, mainly because the team wasn’t professional initially. But the Asian Games gold medal changed the concept of women cricket in Pakistan and parents started encouraging their daughters to go for cricket. Mir said in her interview that ‘she is very thankful that her parents have always supported her. She still remember that she was preparing for chemistry paper and her mother brought her a newspaper which published a print-ad about trials being held to select women’s team and encouraged her to appear in the trial’.



  • Famous Years of Sana Mir

  1. On 4th May, 2009 Mir was handed the Pakistan captaincy for the women World T20. She had formally been vice captain under Urooj Mumtaz, who remained in squad.
    Mir retained her captaincy for the ICC Women’s Cricket Challenges in South Africa.

  2. At the 2010 Asian Games, Mir lead the team to a gold medal.

  3. In 2011, by winning the National Championship for the fourth time in a row, Sana Mir has become the most successful captain of Pakistan at domestic level. Sana also led
    Pakistan team to their first ever tournament with in both the T20 and ODI formats when they played in Sri Lanka in 2011.

  4. Pakistan Women Cricket team has qualified for the World Cup T20 in 2012 and 2013 Women’s ODI World Cup under her captaincy. For the first time the team also beat South
    Africa, improve the world ranking from 8 to 6.
